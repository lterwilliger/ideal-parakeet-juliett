The UNA Library Room Availability Application

Introduction:

The UNA Library has several study and media rooms where students can rent for a period of time. The feature is great for students who are looking to get together and work on group projects or study for that next exam. On the desktop version, this app seems to work very well since all the information can be displayed on the screen at one time. As pictured below, you can select a room number, then follow along horizonitally to find an available date and time for your room. This process is very visual and allows for a fairly standard way to display a date/time confirmation.

Problems:

Although the desktop version has good usability for most users, the mobile version does not. Some major issues arise for students who wish to book their study reservation online. These include accessiblity options being visible, the room number being cut off, and the starting date-time alignment.

These problems can make it nearly impossible to book a reservation on mobile devices. Without clicking an additional link, it is impossible to differentiate between rooms that start with the first two numbers such as "Study room 350" and "Study room 352."

Solutions:

To combat these mobile issues:

  • The info graphic was removed
  • The naming convention was shorted
  • The accessiblity icons were shifted over
  • The starting date-time alignment was moved to first available time
